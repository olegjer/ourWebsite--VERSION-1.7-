Archery

Possibly overlooked when thinking of ways to improve fitness, archery is not one to ignore. It’s considered moderate-to-vigorous exercise, focusing mainly on the upper-body. The biggest hurdle would be the equipment required to take part, that equipment being a drawstring-bow, arrows, and a safe target to shoot for. While this can prove quite expensive, there are many ranges and centres where a beginner can go to get a feel for it, meaning that equipment only needs be bought by those who are already hooked and looking to get in more practice at home. It’s estimated to burn around 200-300 per hour, but with no official time limit that number can be pushed further by extended play. The main physical improvements archers will accrue are greater strength across their arms, upper-back, core, and hands. It’s also an excellent way to improve your hand-eye coordination. So, it might not be the first thing to come to mind when looking to stay fit, archery can absolutely help you shoot for your fitness goals!
