Rugby

Rugby is an incredibly popular sport. It’s a moderate-to vigorous workout, with multiple iterations. Full contact is the most well-known iteration of the sport, but there are less intense ways to play, such as touch rugby. The game technically only requires a ball and the space to play, making it a very accessible sport to get into. Touch rugby burns around 450-600 calories an hour, with a full contact game burning around 600-800 calories an hour, so with a full game lasting 80 minutes, a game of touch rugby burns around 600-800 calories and a full contact game burns around 800-1000 calories. Rugby improves muscle function and growth within its players, as well as bone health and balance. It also contributes to greater cardiovascular, respiratory, and metabolic functions. Being a team-sport, it promotes collaboration and can foster better teamworking skills between players. Whether it’s touch rugby you’re interested in or you’re daring enough to try the full contact game, rugby is a great way to manage your fitness whilst improving yourself mentally as well!
