Tennis

Tennis is one of Britain’s favourite sports. It’s a full-body workout, considered moderate-to-vigorous exercise, and is open to all skill levels. Playing tennis does require a few things, namely a tennis ball, a pair of racquets, and a net in between the players. It’s estimated to burn around 420-600 calories in an hour, but with such a variety of game lengths it can average to a lot more than that. This makes it a great way to burn body fat, as well as lower your resting heartrate and blood pressure, improving cardiovascular and metabolic functions as a whole. As well as that, it improves aerobic capacities, reaction times, flexibility, strength, and muscle tone in its players. Like all exercise, it can also contribute to a better mental wellbeing, reducing stress levels in players. The best part about tennis is that it can vary both between being a team-sport in a doubles game, or an individual one in a singles game, giving you the freedom to choose how you want to play. If you’re interested in a fun way to stay in shape, give tennis a try, you just might love it!
